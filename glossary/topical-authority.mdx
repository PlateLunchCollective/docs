# Topical Authority

**Topical authority** is the depth of coverage and credibility a brand or domain has established on a specific subject area. Search engines and AI retrieval systems both use topical authority signals to determine which sources are most qualified to answer questions on a given topic.

A brand with high topical authority on a subject gets retrieved more consistently, cited more frequently, and represented more accurately by AI systems when that subject comes up in a query.

## How Topical Authority Works

Search and AI systems evaluate topical authority by looking at the breadth and depth of content a source has published on a topic, the quality of citations that content has earned, and how consistently the source has covered the topic over time.

A single comprehensive article doesn't establish topical authority. A sustained body of work — covering the topic from multiple angles, answering the specific questions your audience asks, earning citations from other sources in the space — does.

## Topical Authority vs. Domain Authority

Domain authority is a measure of a site's overall link profile strength. Topical authority is more specific — it measures how well a site covers a particular subject.

A high domain authority site with thin topical coverage will lose AI retrieval ground to a lower domain authority site with deep, well-structured content on the specific topic being queried. For AI SEO, topical authority matters more than it ever did in traditional SEO.

## Building Topical Authority for AI Retrieval

**Cover the full question landscape** — map every question your audience asks about your topic and ensure your content answers each one clearly and directly.

**Go deeper than competitors** — surface-level content doesn't build authority. The goal is to be the most useful resource on the topic, not just present.

**Earn citations within the topic** — other sources in your space linking to and mentioning your content on this topic is the strongest topical authority signal.

**Maintain consistency** — publishing on a topic for two years carries more weight than publishing heavily for two months and stopping.
