# Structured Data

**Structured data** is code added to a webpage that explicitly describes the content to search engines and AI systems. Rather than leaving a crawler to infer what a page is about, structured data states it directly — this is a product, this is its price, this is an FAQ, these are the questions and answers it contains.

The most common format is JSON-LD, a block of JavaScript placed in the page's HTML that uses vocabulary from Schema.org to describe the page's content in a standardized way.

## Why It Matters for AI Retrieval

AI systems favor content they can parse cleanly. Structured data removes ambiguity — it tells the retrieval layer exactly what type of content it's reading, what entities are involved, and what questions the content answers.

For [AEO](/glossary/aeo) specifically, structured data is one of the highest-leverage technical investments a brand can make. FAQ schema, HowTo schema, and Article schema all directly improve the likelihood that content gets extracted and cited in AI-generated responses.

## Schema Types That Matter Most for AI SEO

**Organization** — defines your brand as an entity: name, URL, logo, founding date, social profiles, contact information. This feeds [entity optimization](/glossary/entity-optimization) and knowledge graph signals directly.

**FAQPage** — marks up question-and-answer content so retrieval systems can extract individual Q&A pairs. One of the most direct AEO signals available.

**Article / BlogPosting** — identifies content as editorial, establishes authorship, and signals publication date for recency evaluation.

**Service** — describes a specific service offering, its provider, and its area of coverage. Directly relevant for service business retrieval.

**LocalBusiness** — for businesses with a physical presence or defined service area, this schema feeds local retrieval signals across both search and AI platforms.

**BreadcrumbList** — helps AI systems understand site structure and content hierarchy.

## Implementation Notes

JSON-LD is the recommended format — it keeps structured data separate from visible content and is easiest to maintain. Place it in the `<head>` of each page or just before the closing `</body>` tag.

Every service page, FAQ page, and about page on a brand's site should have relevant schema. It's not optional infrastructure for AI SEO — it's table stakes.
