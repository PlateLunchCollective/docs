# RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation

**Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** is the process by which AI systems fetch external information at query time and use it to generate more accurate, current, and grounded responses.

Without RAG, a language model can only draw on what it learned during training. With RAG, it can pull fresh information from the web, a knowledge base, or a curated index — and incorporate that into its answer.

Most major AI platforms use some form of RAG. When Perplexity searches the web before answering, that's RAG. When ChatGPT browses a URL you provide, that's RAG. When Claude retrieves cited sources in a response, that's RAG.

## Why It Matters for Brand Visibility

RAG is the mechanism that determines which sources get pulled into an AI-generated answer. If your content is structured clearly, cited by authoritative sources, and indexed by the platforms AI systems retrieve from — you have a chance of being included. If it isn't, the model has no way to find you at query time, regardless of how good your content is.

This is why [AEO](/glossary/aeo) and [GEO](/glossary/geo) exist as disciplines. Both are fundamentally about making your brand and content retrievable by RAG systems.

## What RAG Retrieves From

Different platforms pull from different sources:

- Live web search results
- Curated knowledge bases
- Structured data feeds
- Social platforms and forums
- Publisher partnerships and licensed content

The sources vary by platform, but the principle is consistent: content that is findable, structured, and cited gets retrieved. Content that isn't, doesn't.
