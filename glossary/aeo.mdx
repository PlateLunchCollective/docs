# AEO — Answer Engine Optimization

**Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)** is the practice of structuring content so that AI-powered answer systems can accurately extract, interpret, and surface it in response to user queries.

Where traditional SEO optimizes for ranking in a list of links, AEO optimizes for being the direct answer — the content that gets pulled into an AI Overview, a featured snippet, a ChatGPT response, or a Perplexity citation.

## What AEO Targets

AEO is relevant across any platform that generates answers rather than returning a list of URLs:

- Google AI Overviews
- ChatGPT (browsing mode and plugin retrieval)
- Perplexity AI
- Claude
- Gemini
- Bing Copilot
- Voice assistants (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant)

## How It Works

**Question-and-answer formatting** — writing content that directly answers the specific questions your audience is asking, in the language they use to ask them.

**Schema markup** — structured data (FAQ, HowTo, Article, Organization) that tells retrieval systems what type of content they're reading and what questions it answers.

**Entity clarity** — ensuring your brand, products, and subject matter are described with enough specificity that AI systems can distinguish them from similar entities and accurately represent them.

**Concise extractable answers** — AI systems favor content that gets to the point. Long preambles before key answers reduce extraction accuracy.

## AEO vs. Traditional SEO

Traditional SEO is built around keyword ranking signals — backlinks, domain authority, on-page optimization. AEO is built around answer quality signals — structure, specificity, and how cleanly a system can extract a useful response.

Both matter. AEO doesn't replace traditional SEO; it extends it into the retrieval layer that traditional SEO doesn't reach.

## AEO vs. GEO

[GEO](/glossary/geo) focuses on brand authority signals — causing AI systems to recognize, trust, and recommend your brand by name. AEO focuses on content extraction — causing AI systems to pull from your content when answering specific questions.

In practice, both are required. A brand with strong GEO but poor AEO structure may be recognized but not cited. A brand with strong AEO but weak GEO may have content retrieved but lack the authority signals that cause consistent recommendation.
