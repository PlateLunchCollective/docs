# AI Traffic

**AI traffic** is website traffic that originates from AI platforms — users who clicked a link cited in a ChatGPT response, a Perplexity answer, a Claude recommendation, or a similar AI-generated output.

It's a growing traffic source that most analytics setups are not yet configured to capture accurately, which means most brands are underreporting it significantly.

## Why It's Hard to Measure

AI platforms don't always pass referrer data the way traditional search engines do. A click from a Perplexity citation may show up in Google Analytics as direct traffic rather than referral traffic. A ChatGPT link click may appear as organic or go unattributed entirely.

This creates a measurement gap: brands may be receiving meaningful AI-referred traffic without knowing it, and the absence of visible AI traffic in reports doesn't mean AI retrieval isn't happening.

## How to Track It

**UTM parameters on owned content** — if you're publishing content specifically optimized for AI citation, adding UTM parameters to the URLs you want cited gives you a direct signal when those URLs are clicked from AI platforms.

**Referral source filtering** — set up specific referral filters in GA4 for known AI platform domains: perplexity.ai, chat.openai.com, claude.ai, gemini.google.com, and bing.com/chat. Traffic from these sources is AI-referred.

**Direct traffic analysis** — a sustained increase in direct traffic, particularly to pages optimized for AI retrieval, can indicate AI traffic that isn't being attributed correctly.

**Query testing** — running structured queries across AI platforms and recording when your URLs appear in citations gives you a qualitative picture of retrieval even when click-through data is incomplete.

## AI Traffic vs. AI Retrieval

AI traffic measures clicks. AI retrieval measures appearances — how often your brand shows up in AI-generated responses, whether or not the user clicks through.

Both matter. A brand can have high retrieval rates and low AI traffic if AI platforms mention it without linking. Tracking both gives a complete picture of AI visibility.
