# Entity Optimization

**Entity optimization** is the practice of ensuring that AI systems and search engines have a clear, consistent, and accurate understanding of who your brand is, what it does, and how it relates to other entities in your space.

An entity, in this context, is any distinct thing that can be uniquely identified — a brand, a person, a product, a place, a concept. Google's Knowledge Graph, Wikipedia, Wikidata, and the training data of large language models all organize information around entities rather than keywords.

## Why It Matters

When an AI system generates a response about your category, it draws on its understanding of the entities involved. If your brand is a well-defined entity with consistent signals across the web, the system can represent you accurately. If your brand entity is sparse, inconsistent, or confused with similar entities, the system either ignores you or misrepresents you.

Entity optimization is the foundation that [GEO](/glossary/geo) and [AEO](/glossary/aeo) build on. You can't optimize for retrieval if the system doesn't have a reliable model of what you are.

## What Entity Optimization Involves

**Consistency across surfaces** — your brand name, description, location, founding date, and category should be identical everywhere they appear: your website, Google Business Profile, LinkedIn, industry directories, and any structured data markup.

**Knowledge panel signals** — Wikipedia presence, Wikidata records, and Google Knowledge Graph entries all strengthen entity definition. Not every brand needs a Wikipedia article, but the signals that feed knowledge panels matter.

**Schema markup** — Organization, LocalBusiness, Person, and Product schema tell search and AI systems exactly what kind of entity you are and what your key attributes are.

**Authoritative mentions** — citations from sources that AI systems trust help establish entity credibility. A brand mentioned consistently in industry publications becomes a more reliable entity than one that only appears on its own website.
