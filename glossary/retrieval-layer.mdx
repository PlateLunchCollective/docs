# Retrieval Layer

**The retrieval layer** is the system an AI platform uses to find, evaluate, and pull external information before generating a response. It sits between the user's query and the model's output — determining what sources get consulted, what content gets extracted, and what gets included in the answer.

Understanding the retrieval layer is the starting point for understanding why traditional SEO doesn't fully translate to AI visibility. Google's ranking algorithm and an AI platform's retrieval logic are different systems with different inputs, different signals, and different outputs.

## How It Works

When a user submits a query to an AI platform that uses [RAG](/glossary/rag), the retrieval layer activates before the model generates its response. It searches indexed sources, evaluates relevance and credibility, pulls the most useful content, and passes it to the model as context.

The model then generates a response grounded in that retrieved content — which is why the sources the retrieval layer selects directly determine what gets said, what gets cited, and which brands get mentioned.

## What the Retrieval Layer Evaluates

Different platforms weight different signals, but common factors include:

**Source authority** — is the content coming from a source the platform trusts?

**Content structure** — is the content organized in a way that makes extraction clean and accurate?

**Entity clarity** — does the content clearly identify the entities it's describing?

**Recency** — for time-sensitive queries, how current is the content?

**Citation density** — is this source itself cited by other sources the platform trusts?

## Why It Matters for AI SEO

[AEO](/glossary/aeo) and [GEO](/glossary/geo) are both, at their core, retrieval layer strategies. AEO optimizes your content so the retrieval layer can extract it accurately. GEO builds the authority signals that cause the retrieval layer to select your brand in the first place.

If your brand isn't being retrieved, no amount of generative quality in your content matters. The retrieval layer never passes it to the model.
