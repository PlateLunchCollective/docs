# Citation Architecture

**Citation architecture** is the deliberate structure of mentions, links, and references that establish your brand as a credible, retrievable entity across the surfaces that AI systems draw from.

It's not link building in the traditional SEO sense — though backlinks are part of it. Citation architecture is broader: it includes every surface where your brand is mentioned, described, or referenced, and how consistently and authoritatively those mentions signal your expertise and relevance.

## Why Architecture, Not Just Citations

Individual citations are useful. A coherent architecture is what moves the needle for AI retrieval.

AI systems don't evaluate citations in isolation. They look at patterns — whether your brand appears consistently across authoritative sources in your category, whether the descriptions of your brand align across surfaces, whether the entities citing you are themselves credible.

A hundred low-quality directory listings don't build the same signal as ten well-placed mentions in publications your audience actually reads. Citation architecture is the practice of building the right pattern, not just accumulating volume.

## What Citation Architecture Includes

**Tier 1 citations** — industry publications, authoritative news sources, and recognized directories in your vertical. These carry the most weight with AI retrieval systems.

**Tier 2 citations** — trade associations, partner sites, podcast appearances, guest content, and niche directories. These build topical depth and category association.

**Social citations** — Reddit threads, LinkedIn posts, YouTube mentions, and forum discussions. Many AI platforms retrieve from these surfaces directly. They're not optional.

**Owned citations** — your own content citing your own expertise. Internal linking, structured FAQ content, and schema markup all contribute to the citation picture AI systems build of your brand.

## Citation Architecture and RAG

[RAG](/glossary/rag) systems retrieve from the sources they trust most. Citation architecture is the work of becoming one of those sources — or being consistently referenced by them.
