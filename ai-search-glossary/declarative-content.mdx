## Definition

Declarative content is content structured around direct, unambiguous statements of fact — asserting what is true rather than hedging, contextualizing, or qualifying before committing to a claim. It prioritizes clarity of assertion over nuance of framing.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

AI systems extract claims. Declarative content gives them clear, unambiguous claims to extract. Content that hedges every statement — "it could be argued that," "some might suggest," "in certain contexts" — produces claims that AI systems treat as low-confidence and pass over in favor of more assertive sources. This does not mean sacrificing accuracy; it means stating accurate things directly and putting qualifications after the claim rather than before it.
