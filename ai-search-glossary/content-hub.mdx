## Definition

A content hub is a centralized section of a website that organizes all content related to a specific topic — including pillar pages, cluster articles, research reports, glossary entries, and related resources — into a structured, interconnected architecture.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

Content hubs make topical authority visible to AI systems at the structural level. When AI crawlers find a coherent collection of deeply interconnected content on a single topic — all cross-linked, consistently structured, and collectively comprehensive — they register the site as an authoritative source on that topic. A content hub is the architectural expression of topical authority: not just claiming expertise, but demonstrating it through organized, navigable depth.
