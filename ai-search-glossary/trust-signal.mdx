## Definition

A trust signal is any element of a website, content piece, or brand's digital presence that indicates credibility and reliability to search engines, AI systems, and human users. Trust signals include author credentials, citations to primary sources, structured data, third-party mentions, secure hosting, and consistent accurate information over time.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

AI systems have implicit trust hierarchies derived from their training data and retrieval logic. Sources that accumulate trust signals — author bylines with verifiable credentials, citations to authoritative sources, schema markup confirming entity identity — rank higher in those hierarchies and get cited more often. Trust signals are not a single tactic but a property that emerges from consistently publishing accurate, well-attributed, structured content.
