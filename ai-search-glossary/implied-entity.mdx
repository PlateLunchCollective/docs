*Core concept* · *Entity & Knowledge Graph*

## Definition

An implied entity is an entity that is not explicitly named in a piece of content but can be inferred from context — through pronouns, descriptions, or associated concepts that AI systems can resolve back to a specific entity record.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

Implied entities are a double-edged phenomenon. On the positive side, a brand that is so well-established in a knowledge graph that AI systems can resolve references to it without an explicit name mention has achieved strong entity authority. On the negative side, AI systems can incorrectly resolve an implied entity reference — attributing a claim to the wrong brand. Writing with explicit entity references rather than implied ones reduces misattribution risk and improves the accuracy of AI extraction.
