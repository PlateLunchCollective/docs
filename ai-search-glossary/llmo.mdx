## Definition

LLMO — Large Language Model Optimization — is the practice of optimizing content, entity signals, and brand infrastructure specifically to improve how a brand is represented and cited within LLM-generated outputs. It is the discipline that sits at the intersection of traditional SEO, entity optimization, and content strategy.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

LLMO is what PLC does. It is the recognition that LLMs are now a primary channel through which people discover, evaluate, and form opinions about brands — and that the signals LLMs use to evaluate authority are different from the signals traditional search engines use. LLMO is not a replacement for SEO; it is the layer built on top of it, addressing the retrieval, training corpus, and entity infrastructure questions that traditional SEO does not.
