*Measurement* · *Citation & Visibility Measurement*

## Definition

Mention-to-citation ratio is the proportion of brand mentions in AI-generated responses that include an explicit attribution or citation link — as opposed to mentions that reference the brand without attribution. A high ratio indicates that mentions are converting to visible citations; a low ratio indicates that mentions exist but are not being surfaced with attribution.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

Mention-to-citation ratio distinguishes between AI awareness and AI attribution. A brand can be well-known enough to be referenced in AI responses without being cited explicitly — the response uses the brand's name or incorporates its content without linking back. Improving mention-to-citation ratio requires strengthening entity signals — structured data, sameAs arrays, authoritative third-party presence — that give AI systems the confidence to attribute explicitly rather than reference implicitly.
