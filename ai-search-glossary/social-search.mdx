*Core concept* · *Social Search*

## Definition

Social search is the use of social media platforms — TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, Reddit, Pinterest, LinkedIn — as primary search interfaces, where users enter queries and receive results from platform-native content rather than from traditional web indexes. Social search represents a significant and growing share of total search volume, particularly for product discovery, how-to queries, and opinion-seeking.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

Social search is both a direct discovery channel and a feed for AI retrieval systems. When AI platforms generate answers that incorporate social content, they draw from the same pool of indexed, retrievable social media content that social search surfaces. A brand optimized for social search — with keyword-rich descriptions, entity-explicit profiles, organized content archives, and platform-native SEO — is simultaneously building AI citation presence in the social layer. Social search optimization and social AI optimization are largely the same workstream.
