*Platform* · *Entity & Knowledge Graph*

## Definition

Google's Knowledge Graph is Google's proprietary knowledge base of entities and their relationships — used to power Knowledge Panels, AI Overviews, and semantic search features. It contains billions of facts about people, places, organizations, and concepts, drawn from authoritative web sources, Wikipedia, Wikidata, and structured data.

## Why It Matters for AI Search

Google's Knowledge Graph is the most commercially significant knowledge graph for brands. A brand that is well-represented in the Google Knowledge Graph — with accurate entity attributes, verified relationships, and clear category associations — earns Knowledge Panel display, AI Overview citation priority, and more accurate semantic search matching. Knowledge Graph presence is built through the same entity optimization stack as other AI SEO work: structured data, Wikidata, Wikipedia, and consistent corroborating sources.
