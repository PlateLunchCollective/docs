# Fractional CMO FAQ

**What does a Fractional CMO actually do in the first 90 days?**

The first sprint begins with a diagnostic review of current marketing activity, team structure, vendor relationships, budget allocation, and business objectives. Strategy and priorities are set based on that review. The Fractional CMO then directs execution through the sprint, coordinates between internal teams and external vendors, and holds everyone accountable to the agreed priorities. At the end of the sprint, findings and recommendations are documented. The deliverable is not a slide deck. It is a functioning marketing operation with a clear direction.

**How is a Fractional CMO different from a marketing agency?**

An agency executes. A Fractional CMO directs. The agency produces the work. The Fractional CMO decides what work gets produced, evaluates whether it is serving the business objective, and coordinates across vendors to ensure the parts add up to something coherent. Businesses that have agencies but no one setting strategy and holding vendors accountable are a common use case for a Fractional CMO engagement.

**What size company benefits most from a Fractional CMO?**

Businesses in the range of $3M to $30M in revenue that have outgrown purely tactical execution but are not ready to hire a full-time CMO. The model also fits businesses navigating a specific transition — a rebrand, a new market entry, a channel shift — where senior marketing judgment is needed for a defined period.

**How do you measure the ROI of a Fractional CMO?**

Against the objectives set at the start of the sprint. Those objectives are specific and measurable — lead volume, conversion rates, cost per acquisition, revenue from a specific channel, or whatever metrics matter most to the business at that stage. The Fractional CMO is accountable to those metrics, not to hours billed or deliverables produced.

**How do I know if a Fractional CMO has the experience to actually help?**

Ask for specific examples of businesses they have worked with at a similar stage and what changed as a result of the engagement. Ask what they would do in the first 30 days at your specific business and why. A Fractional CMO with real experience gives specific, grounded answers. One without it gives generic frameworks. The difference is apparent quickly.

**What does a Fractional CMO engagement typically cost?**

Varies based on scope and time commitment. Sprint-based models are typically priced per sprint rather than hourly. The cost is substantially less than a full-time CMO hire when accounting for salary, benefits, and equity, and the engagement ends cleanly when the sprint is complete rather than requiring severance or a drawn-out offboarding.

**Will a Fractional CMO manage my existing marketing team?**

Yes, if that is part of the scope. Managing and developing an internal team is a common part of a Fractional CMO engagement for businesses that have junior marketing staff but no senior leadership to direct them. The Fractional CMO sets priorities, provides direction, and evaluates work quality. The goal is to leave the team more capable at the end of the engagement than at the start.
